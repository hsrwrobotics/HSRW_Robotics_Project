JRC-AUTOTRAC 2020: A small-scale automated vehicles competition#

The JRC-AUTOTRAC 2020 is a competition for teams of makers who will compete against each other with self-built fleets of small-scale automated vehicles. After postponing it twice due to the effects of the Covid pandemics, the final event of the competition was held in the form of an online event on 17 June 2021 as part of the 7th International IEEE Conference on Models and Technologies for Intelligent Transportation Systems.

HSRW-Hammy Self-driving Vehicles

Teams#

The plan at first is that there would be 12 teams competing together in the physical events on April 2020 in Ispra, Italy. However due to the effects of the Covid pandemic, the competition was postponed for the first time on March 2020, and later was convert to a remote event.

At the time of the final event hosted on June 2021, there are only three international teams left, which are:

  • Technical University of Delft
  • University of California Riverside
  • Rhine-Waal University of Applied Sciences (HSRW-Hammy)

Regulations#

Each team will show the performance of their in-house developed fleet of four small-scale automated vehicles on the two different traffic scenarios prepared for the race, including High-way scenario and Urban Scenario.

During the performance of their vehicles the teams will earn or loose points. The team collecting the highest number of points among the two scenarios will win the competition.
